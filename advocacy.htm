Advocacy for Mobility Aid Recognition

Introduction

I have spent over a decade refining the use of skates as a vital mobility aid to manage my ballistic mobility impairment before addressing the legal and public-access challenges in their acceptance. This page is advocacy: it summarizes accommodation history, disputed access issues, scientific interpretation, and policy goals rather than claiming that every legal question has been finally decided.

SamTrans Incident

After years of successfully using skates as a mobility aid, I reached out to SamTrans to ensure proper accommodation. I believe the response misunderstood or mischaracterized the mobility aid, resulting in a prolonged struggle for recognition. Despite these challenges, I continued to advocate for my rights and the rights of others with similar needs.

Legal Precedents

Through persistent efforts, I obtained court access records in San Mateo Superior Court allowing skates as a mobility aid in that setting. I also built an accommodation history around riding a motorcycle with skates, a practice I had safely maintained for over 18 years before a disputed license suspension.

Data Science Proof

The functional argument for using skates as a mobility aid is supported by data science. My research documents within person differences in heart rate, HRV, duration, mobility mode, and reported burden between walking and skating. These signals do not prove pain by themselves, but they support accommodation analysis when interpreted with the medical history, biomechanics, and activity context.

Discrimination and the Need for Identification

Despite the evidence and access records, myself and others continue to face disputed accommodation barriers regarding our mobility aids. To address this, I advocate for mobility aids like skates to be identified on a driver's license or comparable credential. This would facilitate easier verification and support, helping individuals with unique mobility needs request appropriate accommodations. I am now DMV certified to ride a motorcycle with skates, however my driver's license has a restriction of 50 (RSTR 50) on the front as an indirect reference to contact the DMV for verification.

Due to recurring access disputes, validation should be clearer than an opaque restriction code so a person can explain mobility aid usage for locomotion in business settings and while operating vehicles.

I am proposing that a handicap symbol be displayed on the front of a driver's license indicating a disability, where the back indicates whether they need a mobility aid for locomotion and or driving:
HANDICAP: Use skate prostheses for locomotion and driving

Civil Rights Complaints

In 2006, I filed a Civil Rights complaint with the Department of Justice, which led to US Department of Transportation/Federal Transit Administration review of reasonable-accommodation issues. I believe the transit record relied on incomplete or inaccurate biomechanics assumptions, and the result became a temporary grant of ParaTransit. After years, I am still under a temporary grant of Paratransit, underscoring the ongoing struggle for recognition and equality.

In 2022, my license was suspended during the pandemic after a San Mateo Police Department reexamination request and DMV review that I dispute. I believe the record mischaracterized the safety and accommodation issues, and the suspension impeded my ability to work and contribute to society. I seek recognition of the accommodation history and clearer standards for using skates as a necessary mobility aid.
A new Civil Rights Complaint is currently being written to seek equitable disability rights.
