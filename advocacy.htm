Legal Precedents

Through persistent efforts, I set a legal precedent in San Mateo Superior Court, affirming the right to use skates as a mobility aid. Additionally, I established the precedent of riding a motorcycle with skates, a practice I had safely maintained for over 18 years. This was necessitated after my license was wrongfully suspended due to police disinformation.

Data Science Proof

The necessity of using skates as a mobility aid is backed by comprehensive data science. My research demonstrates significant differences in heart rate and pain levels between walking and skating. This evidence highlights the critical need for skates in managing my condition and underscores the importance of proper accommodation.

Discrimination and the Need for Identification

Despite the clear evidence and legal precedents, myself and others continue to face discrimination regarding our mobility aids. To address this, it is imperative that mobility aids like skates be identified on driver's licenses. This would facilitate easier verification and support, ensuring that individuals with unique mobility needs receive the accommodations they deserve.

Civil Rights Complaint

In 2006, I filed a Civil Rights complaint with the Department of Justice, which led to the US DOT-FTA being mandated to evaluate reasonable accommodations. Despite this, I have been denied access to public transportation since 1991, underscoring the ongoing struggle for recognition and equality.
