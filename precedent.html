IRS

Medical and tax framing

Skates are documented as functional disability equipment, not recreation. This supports the public argument that inline skates can be a non-traditional mobility aid, while leaving formal legal outcomes to the relevant agency or court records.

Courts

Court access

Superior Court access records show prosthetic skates treated as a reasonable accommodation inside courtroom settings. This is an important access record, not a universal ruling for every venue.

DOT / FTA / BART

2007 FTA Letter of Finding

The FTA analyzed whether roller skates used as a mobility aid could be excluded as a direct threat. It concluded BART had not demonstrated direct threat in concourse areas and outside controlled areas, and stated BART must allow use there, subject to reasonable fact-based restrictions.

This does not replace a court ruling, but it is a federal civil-rights determination and should be treated as major administrative evidence.

Read the public FTA determination
DMV

Driving with skates

The DMV updated my record to reflect operation of a motorcycle and Class C vehicle using inline skates.

That matters because it supports the functional reality of prosthetic skates as part of my mobility system. It does not decide every public access question, but it helps rebut the assumption that skates are merely recreation.

Air travel

Recognition failure far from home

The issue was not merely refusal to board. The issue was whether a non-traditional mobility aid would be recognized consistently enough to avoid stranding a disabled traveler far from home.

Public burden

Federal buildings and events

I was forced to remove my prosthetic skates to enter a federal building to file a systemic discrimination complaint. I was later again refused access when trying to meet with a pro bono attorney in a federal building.

The burden is simple: the system required painful walking in order to complain about being forced to walk painfully. Wearable biometrics can help document that burden, but the legal question remains fact-specific.

The same transportation history explains the motorcycle adaptation: when public transportation blocked the accessible path, I had to build another way to reach work, appointments, groceries, court, and public life.

Healthcare relevance

Why healthcare teams should care

This access record is not only a legal history. It is also a wearable-health case study showing how ordinary categories fail when a person’s functional mobility does not match expected labels.

The public record can support better review systems for mobility aids, transportation burden, accommodation requests, and disability-aware health analytics.

Evidence governance

The record separates source documents, interpretation, disputed claims, and platform potential so reviewers can understand what is documented and what still requires review.

Assistive mobility

Non-traditional mobility aids should be evaluated by functional effect, not appearance or assumptions about recreation.

For public standards, doctrine, and accommodation guidance, visit HandicapSkater.org.

Real World Burden

Wearable and route records can help show when forced alternatives create avoidable physiological, logistical, or access burden.

What this does not claim

That business positioning is separate from HandicapSkater.org's nonprofit standards role and does not assert a platform, court, agency, or healthcare endorsement.
