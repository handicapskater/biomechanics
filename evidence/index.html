Source-Linked FSI/CSS Results

FSI, CSS, and Tensor Remain Distinct

Fractal Stability Index (FSI) is used only in its named scope. Cohort Similarity Score (CSS) answers a similarity question under an established cohort-comparison scope. Tensor v0.3 remains the frozen multidimensional model identity.

The publication contract does not authorize a universal cohort ranking or browser-side reconstruction. Values retain their named metrics, units, and metric-specific sample counts.

How to Read the Evidence Corpus

A Review Sequence

  1. Identify the source.Know whether the observation comes from WHOOP, Strava, Polar H10/Kubios, documentary history, or another authority.
  2. Identify the cohort.Confirm the activity, vehicle, body-coupling state, and classification provenance.
  3. Inspect the sample count.Small and unequal samples require explicit qualification.
  4. Inspect the units.Distance, duration, acceleration, ratios, and unitless indexes answer different questions.
  5. Inspect limitations and missingness.Unavailable evidence should remain unavailable.
  6. Compare like with like.Do not collapse output, absolute burden, normalized burden, or similarity into one label.
  7. Separate longitudinal and episodic evidence.Overnight recovery context is not a targeted session measurement.
  8. Keep the conclusion personal.This case may illustrate a review method; it does not establish a universal result.
