Current Corpus Model

Layered Evidence, Not One Flattened Score

The model keeps evidence layers separate long enough to preserve their meaning, then reconciles only approved relationships for review.

  1. Longitudinal contextRepeated activity and recovery patterns across time.
  2. Episodic measurementHigher-specificity session evidence from RRI, HRV, ECG, and acceleration.
  3. Movement cohortWalking and distinct skating contexts with preserved source labels.
  4. Transportation cohortVehicle, passenger or active-control state, posture, and body coupling.
  5. Repeated protocolAccepted stage sequences reviewed without inferring missing membership.
  6. Source reconciliationTime, labels, duplicates, provenance, and authority resolved before interpretation.
  7. ValidationUnits, samples, missingness, parity, safety, and formula identity checked.
  8. Publication layerApproved summaries and graph payloads synchronized to render-only websites.

Validation and Audit Checks

What Must Survive Review

Credibility depends as much on what is rejected, missing, qualified, or source-limited as on the values that are shown.

Source authority

Use the designated source for each field and preserve original labels and provenance.

Duplicate handling

Retain audit history while preventing duplicate observations from silently changing analytical samples.

Time reconciliation

Preserve timestamps, timezone context, and the rules used to relate records across sources.

Sample requirements

Show the applicable sample count and retain explicit small-sample qualifications.

Missingness

Unavailable evidence remains unavailable; it is neither fabricated nor silently treated as zero.

Unit checks

Keep supplied units visible and compare like with like.

Parity checks

Published values must match the server-side Evidence Observatory presentation.

Publication safety

Public bundles exclude raw signals, private identifiers, local paths, and browser scientific computation.

Date and limitations

Every interpretation remains tied to a data-through date and visible limitations.

How to Read the Evidence Corpus

A Review Sequence

  1. Identify the source.Know whether the observation comes from WHOOP, Strava, Polar H10/Kubios, documentary history, or another authority.
  2. Identify the cohort.Confirm the activity, vehicle, body-coupling state, and classification provenance.
  3. Inspect the sample count.Small and unequal samples require explicit qualification.
  4. Inspect the units.Distance, duration, acceleration, ratios, and unitless indexes answer different questions.
  5. Inspect limitations and missingness.Unavailable evidence should remain unavailable.
  6. Compare like with like.Do not collapse output, absolute burden, normalized burden, or similarity into one label.
  7. Separate longitudinal and episodic evidence.Overnight recovery context is not a targeted session measurement.
  8. Keep the conclusion personal.This case may illustrate a review method; it does not establish a universal result.

Why This Matters to Wearable Health and Mobility-Intelligence Teams

Context Is Part of the Signal

Wearable context preservation, mobility classification, multimodal sensor fusion, within-person baselines, longitudinal and episodic integration, transportation context, provenance, explainable evidence systems, and human review all affect whether a mobility record remains meaningful.

This case does not claim a clinically validated product or commercial partnership. It shows a concrete design problem: personalized mobility intelligence should preserve what the person was doing, how the body was coupled to it, where the signal came from, and what the evidence cannot establish.

Evidence boundary

Wearables estimate physiologic, autonomic, musculoskeletal, movement, impact, and transport burden. They do not directly measure pain. Results are within-person evidence, not clinical proof, diagnosis, causation, universal proof, or legal proof.
