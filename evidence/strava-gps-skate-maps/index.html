Strava GPS Skate Maps for Physical Therapy

COVID-era and long-term skate routes document repeated real-world skating used as physical therapy, functional mobility, and mobility-aid context.

What These Maps Show

These maps document repeated real-world skating routes, including distance, route, duration, and practical mobility-aid use. They support the history and feasibility of skating-based mobility and help explain why transportation to and from skating sessions is part of the accommodation context.

  • Route data is activity context, not medical diagnosis.
  • Route maps do not prove pain by themselves.
  • Maps should be read with wearable, HRV/RRI, ParaTransit, biomechanics, and legal accommodation context.
  • The routes help show a functional mobility pattern, not isolated recreation.
  • Distance is functional mobility achieved, not automatic burden.

What a health or mobility reviewer should notice

These routes show repeated, real-world physical therapy skating over time. They document distance, duration, route consistency, and functional mobility. They are not offered as medical diagnosis or proof of pain by themselves. They show the lived movement pattern that wearable and targeted sensor evidence can contextualize.

COVID-era route continuity

Repeated routes show physical therapy skating continued as a practical mobility pattern during a disrupted period.

Long-distance functional mobility

Distance, duration, and route consistency help show that the routes are part of a functional mobility pattern, not isolated recreation.

Why ParaTransit matters

Transportation to and from physical therapy skating is part of the accommodation context, not a separate recreational errand.

Functional Mobility Efficiency

The maps show mobility achieved. The data question is how much physiological and biomechanical burden was required to achieve that mobility.
