HandicapSkater: Evolution for Disability Rights

Driving and Ambulating with Skates Used as a Mobility Aid

Skates have been recognized on my driver’s license as a prosthetic mobility aid, allowing me to legally ride a motorcycle and drive a car. But this status is buried within DMV records and requires insider knowledge to find. My driver's license has a restriction of 50 (RSTR 50) on the front as an indirect reference to contact the DMV for verification that is untenable outside the state.

Due to recurring access disputes, validation should be clearer than an opaque restriction code so a person can explain mobility aid usage for locomotion in business settings and while operating vehicles. I am proposing that a handicap symbol be displayed on the front of a driver's license instead of RSTR 50 indicating a disability, where the back indicates whether they need a mobility aid for locomotion and/or driving: HANDICAP: Ambulates and drives with prosthetic device skates.

This would help anyone using non-standard mobility aids—like skates, adaptive scooters, or wearable robotics—avoid confusion and discrimination from law enforcement, TSA, employers, and transit authorities.

Why Skates? A New Form of Mobility

Walking causes ballistic shock to the joints, especially for those with pelvic or back injuries. Skating dramatically reduces compressive impact, acting like a wheel for the body. It’s not just sport—it’s science.

  • Managing pain and mobility after multiple pelvic fractures and cracked sacroiliac joint
  • Avoiding pain-induced cardiovascular events , such as walking
  • Supporting broader health improvements alongside clinical care
  • Supporting metabolic health through mobility, nutrition, and medical follow-up

Data-Driven Disability Science

HandicapSkater uses AI and biomechanics to analyze:

  • How skates can reduce harmful impact by preserving more usable forward motion between pushes
  • How HRV and cardiovascular data can support within person mobility comparisons
  • When access disputes block necessary aids

Your health data remains private but can support research and accommodation standards through anonymized, carefully interpreted evidence.

Legal Precedents and Civil Disobedience

The record includes accommodation history and disputed legal issues involving:
