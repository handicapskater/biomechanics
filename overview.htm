HandicapSkater: Mobility Innovation for Disability Rights

Driving and Ambulating with Prosthetic Skates

Skates have been recognized on my driver’s license as a prosthetic mobility aid, allowing me to legally ride a motorcycle and drive a car. But this status is buried within DMV records and requires insider knowledge to find.

Add to the back of the license:
HANDICAP: Ambulates and drives with prosthetic device skates.

This would help anyone using non-standard mobility aids—like skates, adaptive scooters, or wearable robotics—avoid confusion and discrimination from law enforcement, TSA, employers, and transit authorities.

Why Skates? A New Form of Mobility

Walking causes ballistic shock to the joints, especially for those with pelvic or back injuries. Skating dramatically reduces compressive impact, acting like a wheel for the body. It’s not just sport—it’s science.

  • Managing pain and mobility after multiple pelvic fractures and cracked sacroiliac joint
  • Avoiding pain-induced cardiovascular events (PICE), such as walking
  • Reversing hypothyroidism without medication
  • Curing fatty liver by combining skating with nutrition

Legal Precedents and Civil Disobedience

I’ve already forced change through:

  • San Mateo and San Francisco Superior Courts granting skate access in the courtroom as a reasonable accommodation
  • Winning a 2007 DOT ruling to skate to physical therapy using ParaTransit
  • Fighting the DMV’s suspension of my license—eventually reinstated for driving with skates
  • Writing legal pleadings pro se to certify skating as a medical and legal necessity
