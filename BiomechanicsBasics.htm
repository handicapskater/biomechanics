Impact Shock
The force produced during a step, at the foot (skate), that propagates throughout the body.

Pelvic Structure


The pelvic girdle supports the HAT and coordinates rotational movement of the upper and lower halves of the body. During a normal walking pattern the upper half of the body rotates right, the lower half rotates left then vice-versa, meanwhile the pelvis is the hinge coordinating this movement. The SI Joints act as shock absorbers to absorb the impact shocks of each step as it flows through the lumbo-sacro-pelvic region.

closed kinematic chain


Norkin and Levangie (p. 68) describe the function of Joints as the ability to establish stability in the human body and then to focus on providing mobility. In the pelvis, a closed kinematic chain exists around the pelvic girdle. Minute movements involving the pelvis can cause stresses around the pelvic girdle. A kinematic chain produces a skill or movement through the combination of various joints and their degrees of freedom (Hamill and Knutzen, p. 25).
