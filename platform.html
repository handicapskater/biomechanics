Wearable Mobility Evidence Platform

A research prototype for organizing wearable, route, and targeted sensor evidence into user-controlled mobility records.

Research and Platform Scope

The commercial opportunity is framed as strategic fit, licensing, research, standards, or platform collaboration after validation for wearable health data science, health AI, accessibility, consumer wearable ecosystems, and health data interoperability. This is not a claim of any current platform partnership.

What this can become

A user-controlled mobility evidence layer that helps people organize wearable data, route data, targeted sensor sessions, and accommodation events into privacy preserving summaries for clinicians, accessibility teams, agencies, employers, and courts.

This is not a diagnostic product. It is a research prototype for organizing functional mobility evidence so reviewers can evaluate mobility by context, burden, and function instead of appearance.

Wearable biometrics

HR, HRV/RMSSD, activity labels, duration, route context, and recovery signals organized as user-controlled evidence.

Mobility analytics

Walking, skating, wheelchair use, ParaTransit, motorcycle travel, commuting, and forced alternatives treated as different functional contexts.

Pattern detection

Within-person patterns can support accommodation analysis when interpreted with medical history, biomechanics, and user context.

Core modules

activity label reconciliation

Preserve source labels while documenting user-confirmed mobility context.

source linked evidence

Keep wearable, route, sensor, and legal-access records traceable to source artifacts.

Duplicate-aware recordkeeping

Separate analytical exclusions from audit retention so review remains transparent.

Mobility intelligence

Compare mobility modes by functional burden rather than generic fitness categories.

Evidence Boundaries

HR and HRV do not prove pain by themselves. FSI and CSS are proposed synthesized metrics for functional stress and cumulative strain; they are not medical diagnoses, clinical validation, legal rulings, or guaranteed accommodations.
