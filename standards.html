Nonprofit Standards Scope

The nonprofit mission is distinct from HandicapSkater.com's commercial IP. It focuses on fair access, public sector education, and evidence-based accommodation standards.

Mobility aid standards

Standards for evaluating non-traditional mobility aids by function, safety, context, and disability need rather than appearance.

Accommodation education

Plain-language resources for agencies, employers, venues, and courts reviewing adaptive mobility documentation.

Supportive documentation

Wearable biometrics can support accommodation narratives when presented as within-person evidence, not standalone diagnosis.

Draft standards for wearable mobility evidence

  1. Preserve raw source labels.
  2. Disclose surrogate labels.
  3. Retain duplicates for audit.
  4. Exclude baseline/recovery records from burden comparisons.
  5. Separate route evidence from physiology evidence.
  6. Avoid single-metric pain claims.
  7. Require reviewer confirmation for ambiguous activity labels.
  8. Evaluate mobility aids by function, not appearance.
  9. Distinguish user-defined labels from platform-default labels.

What this does not claim

The public record includes court access records, administrative determinations, accommodation history, scientific interpretation, and disputed or pending issues. This framework does not claim guaranteed accommodation, final legal victory, or legal advice.
