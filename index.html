HandicapSkater

A disability access record showing why inline skates can function as a non-standard mobility aid.

Walking is controlled falling. For my pelvic, sacroiliac, hip, and spine impairment, repeated step-catch walking creates pain and physiologic burden. Inline skating changes the movement pathway into controlled rolling, allowing functional mobility where walking becomes unsustainable.

This site organizes the story, pain basis, biomechanics, evidence corpus, transportation burden, and legal access record behind that claim.

Visual evidence

What Looks Like a Stunt Is the Access Story

A short public video shows the core HandicapSkater problem in one scene: shopping at Smart & Final, leaving the store on skates, rolling to a motorcycle, and riding away with skates still on.

Many people see a stunt. The record shows something different: a disabled person using skates as a mobility aid because walking, standing, public transit, and vehicle access all create functional barriers.

This is the point of the site: function must be reviewed before appearance.

Why it matters

Function Before Appearance

Inline skates are commonly seen as recreation. In this record, they function as a mobility aid. The access question is not whether the device looks ordinary. The question is whether it changes movement, reduces functional burden, can be accommodated safely, and preserves access.
