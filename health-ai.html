Two-Entity Strategy: .com and .org

HandicapSkater separates commercial platform rights from nonprofit standards work so the business opportunity and the civil-rights mission remain clear.

HandicapSkater.com

Commercial Platform

HandicapSkater.com is positioned as the commercial AI/ML wearable data science platform: wearable biometrics, HR/HRV/activity evidence, mobility accommodation analytics, and pattern detection for non-standard mobility contexts.

The commercial rights may be a strategic fit, licensing candidate, acquisition candidate, or platform extension for organizations working in Google Health, Fitbit, Android Health, accessibility, health AI, and wearable data science. That is business positioning and future potential, not a claim of partnership.

HandicapSkater.org

Nonprofit Standards

HandicapSkater.org is framed as the nonprofit standards, civil-rights, and accommodation framework: non-traditional mobility aid standards, evidence-based accommodation standards, public-sector accessibility education, and fair-access advocacy.

The nonprofit role is distinct from the commercial IP. It can use wearable biometrics as supportive documentation while keeping the focus on disability rights, public access, and standards that agencies can understand.

Wearable Evidence for Accommodation

HR and HRV do not prove pain by themselves. They can be affected by exertion, stress, sleep, medication, temperature, hydration, illness, and measurement quality.

  • Within-person patterns across HR, HRV/RMSSD, duration, mobility mode, route context, and biomechanical load can support accommodation evidence.
  • FSI and CSS are proposed synthesized metrics for functional stress and cumulative strain. They are not medical diagnoses and should be interpreted with clinical and factual context.
  • The public proof layer distinguishes record facts, scientific interpretation, business positioning, and future potential.

Why This Matters for Google/Fitbit/Health

The strategic fit is the pattern-recognition problem: wearable platforms can move beyond generic fitness categories toward disability-aware mobility analytics, privacy-preserving evidence summaries, and health AI that recognizes adaptive movement.

Fitbit-style signals

HR, HRV, recovery, activity labels, duration, and movement smoothness can help identify when a mobility option is sustainable or harmful for a specific person.

Android Health context

Phone and wearable ecosystems can organize user-controlled records across mobility modes, routes, and accommodation events without treating step count as the only good outcome.

Accessibility product potential

The platform potential is a disability-aware evidence layer for users, clinicians, employers, transit agencies, and courts. It is not a claim that Google or Fitbit has endorsed the work.
