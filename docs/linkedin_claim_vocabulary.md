# LinkedIn Claim Vocabulary

LinkedIn company copy should use the same claim vocabulary as
HandicapSkater.com and the cross-repo source map.

## Internal Claim IDs

- `HS-CLAIM-001`: skating as documented functional mobility support.
- `HS-CLAIM-002`: walking burden can be disproportionate to distance.
- `HS-CLAIM-003`: ParaTransit bus/cutaway and van can create passive mechanical
  burden in the source-linked record.
- `HS-CLAIM-004`: WHOOP, Strava, Kubios/H10, legal records, and reports have
  distinct evidence roles.
- `HS-CLAIM-005`: FSI/CSS/RAG support reviewer-safe decision support.
- `HS-CLAIM-006`: `.com`, `.org`, datascience, and platform repos have distinct
  roles and data boundaries.

## Public Copy Vocabulary

Use:

- source linked evidence
- within person pattern
- functional mobility
- mobility burden
- evidence organization
- reviewer safe summaries
- corroborates the broader record
- qualified reviewers

Avoid:

- medical diagnosis
- proves pain
- final legal victory
- guaranteed accommodation
- agency endorsement
- court endorsement
- current platform partnership
- automated entitlement

LinkedIn posts do not need to display claim IDs, but drafts should map each
claim to one or more IDs before publication.
