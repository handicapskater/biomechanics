In 1983, I was in an accident that left me disabled. The following kinematics model depicts how I suffered my injuries.
 
My Orthopedic Surgeon explained that I had a ballistic mobility impairment and to avoid ballisticMotions, such as running. Furthermore, the Surgeon stated that I had a limited number of impactShocks, before surgery would be needed.
The injuries that I sustained are listed below:
  1. Left superior ramus proximally in the acetabular region [fracture]
  2. A fracture near the left ischiopubic synchondrosis
  3. A right parasymphyseal fracture [later diagnosed as a fracture in the pubic symphysis]
  4. A right pubic fracture near the ischiopubic synchondrosis
  5. Pubic diastasis
  6. Head of the left femur is a little higher than the right because of a pelvic deformity ~1 cm
  7. There is a widening of the left compared to the right [later diagnosed as a cracked sacroiliac joint]
  8. Evidence of sacrum diastasis
diastasis - a force injury to bones without a fracture.
ischiopubic - relating to both ischium and pubis.
ramus - branch like structure.
synchondrosis - an immovable articulation in which the union is formed by cartilage.
